## Probability: Bayes Theorem
P(A|B) = (P(B|A) * P(A)) / P(B).
Where P(A|B) is the probability of A given B is true.

## Calculus: Derivatives
d/dx(x^n) = n*x^(n-1)
d/dx(sin x) = cos x
d/dx(e^x) = e^x
d/dx(ln x) = 1/x

## Calculus: Integration
Integral of x^n dx = (x^(n+1))/(n+1) + C (for n != -1)

## Linear Algebra: Determinants
For matrix [[a, b], [c, d]], determinant = ad - bc.
If det = 0, matrix is singular.

## Domain Constraints
- Denominators cannot be zero.
- Square roots of negative numbers are complex (not real).
- Probability must be between 0 and 1.
